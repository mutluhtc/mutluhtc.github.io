About

Research, teaching, and practical problem solving

My academic work centers on structures arising in representation theory, category theory, and equivariant mathematics. I am particularly interested in turning abstract constructions into clear definitions, usable formulas, and carefully organized proofs.

Alongside research, I have extensive university teaching experience and enjoy designing explanations that help students move from intuition to rigorous understanding. My technical projects also include machine learning, natural-language processing, and educational software.

Research

Current mathematical interests

Selected themes from my ongoing and recent work. Publication details can be added when the corresponding papers and preprints are public.

01

Mackey 2-functors

Categorical forms of induction, restriction, base change, and Frobenius-type structures in equivariant settings.

02

Categorified plus constructions

Upper and lower constructions for turning restriction data into richer Mackey-theoretic structures.

03

Mark transformations

Categorical analogues of classical mark maps, including explicit component and double-coset formulas.
